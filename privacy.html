3. Key terms (DPA Section 2)

  • Personal data — information identifying or relating to an identifiable individual (e.g. name, NRC number, phone number, bank details).
  • Sensitive personal data — includes financial information, employment details, and other categories defined in Section 14 of the Act.
  • Data subject — the individual the data is about (you, our customer or applicant).
  • Data Controller — the entity that determines why and how personal data is processed (Goodfellow Finance Limited).
  • Processing — any operation on personal data including collection, storage, use, disclosure or deletion.
  • Consent — a freely given, specific, informed and unambiguous indication of your agreement to processing (Section 15).

6. Sensitive personal data

Loan processing requires financial and employment-related personal data. We process this data only for lending, recovery, regulatory compliance and related legitimate purposes. We apply additional safeguards including restricted access, staff confidentiality obligations and secure storage systems.

We do not sell your personal data to third parties.

7. Sharing & disclosure

We may share personal data with:

  • Your employer — for PMEC payroll deduction authorisation and verification
  • Your bank — for DDACC direct debit setup, disbursement and recovery
  • Mobile network operators — for *774# USSD and mobile money services
  • Credit reference bureaus — for credit checks and reporting as permitted by law
  • Bank of Zambia and other regulators — when required by law
  • Professional advisers and auditors — under confidentiality obligations
  • IT and service providers — who process data on our behalf under written agreements
  • Law enforcement or courts — when required by law or court order

We require all third-party processors to protect personal data in line with the Data Protection Act, 2021.

8. Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes collected:

  • Active loan records: for the duration of the loan and recovery period
  • Closed loan records: up to 7 years after final settlement, as required for financial and regulatory record-keeping
  • Unsuccessful applications: up to 12 months unless you consent to longer retention
  • Marketing data: until you withdraw consent or opt out
  • Complaint records: as required by Bank of Zambia complaint-handling requirements
  • Website logs: typically up to 12 months

When data is no longer needed, we securely delete or anonymise it.

9. Security

We implement technical and organisational measures to protect personal data, including:

  • SSL/TLS encryption on our website
  • Access controls limiting data to authorised staff on a need-to-know basis
  • Secure branch procedures for handling physical documents
  • Staff confidentiality and data protection training
  • Regular review of our information security practices

10. Data-breach notification

If a personal data breach occurs that is likely to result in a risk to your rights, we will notify the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner within 24 hours as required by Section 49 of the Act. Where the breach is likely to result in a high risk to you, we will also notify affected data subjects without undue delay.

11. Your rights as a data subject

Under the Data Protection Act, 2021, you have the right to:

  • Access — request confirmation of whether we process your data and obtain a copy
  • Rectification — request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Erasure — request deletion where legally permitted
  • Object — object to processing, including direct marketing
  • Withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, at any time
  • Complain — lodge a complaint with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner if you believe your rights have been infringed

To exercise your rights, email customercare@goodfellow.co.zm or write to our DPO at the head office address above. We will respond within the timeframe prescribed by the Act.

Office of the Data Protection Commissioner: www.dataprotection.gov.zm
